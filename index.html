Project Work

Below is the list of project I have worked on

Inventory Lookup and Reporting Application

During my time as an Inventory Administrative Support staff member at PT. Euroasiatic Jaya, inventory records were managed mostly through manual processes. The company used a simple desktop program that functioned mainly as a digital stock card and allowed only basic stock lookup and reports. To obtain more detailed information or customised reports, data often had to be processed manually using spreadsheets, which was time-consuming and prone to errors.

To improve this process, I independently designed and developed a simple desktop application using Visual Basic and SQL based on the organisation’s existing inventory data. The application allows users to search inventory records, view stock movement data, and generate customised reports through a user-friendly interface. By integrating the original database with manual records previously maintained in spreadsheets, the system made it easier to retrieve stock information and prepare inventory reports using a single application.

Reflective Learning

What I learned so far

Business Professionalism in the Workplace

One important concept I learned during my studies at ICL is business professionalism. Business professionalism refers to how people behave and communicate in a professional work environment. It includes showing respect to colleagues, communicating clearly, taking responsibility for tasks, and following ethical standards at work.

Through this learning, I realised that professionalism is not only about dressing formally or following rules. It is also about building trust and maintaining respectful relationships in the workplace. In some organisations, businesses may focus strongly on profit and performance targets. However, if professionalism is not maintained, the work environment may become stressful or uncomfortable for employees.

This learning helped me understand that professionalism can support a positive workplace culture. When employees treat each other respectfully and communicate professionally, teamwork becomes more effective. It can also improve employee satisfaction and cooperation within the organisation.

In my future career, I plan to apply this learning by maintaining professional behaviour in my work. For example, I will communicate respectfully with colleagues, complete my responsibilities carefully, and act honestly in my work. I also hope to contribute to a workplace environment where employees feel respected and supported. By practicing professionalism, I believe I can build good working relationships and contribute positively to the organisation.

Management Behaviour and Employee Motivation

During my studies in organisational behaviour, I learned about how management behaviour can influence employee motivation and job satisfaction. Managers play an important role in guiding and supporting employees in their work. The way managers interact with employees can affect how motivated employees feel at work.

One example discussed in class was micromanagement. Micromanagement happens when managers control every small detail of employees’ work. When employees are not given enough independence, they may feel that they are not trusted. This can reduce their motivation and confidence in their work.

This concept helped me understand why some organisations experience problems such as low employee motivation or high employee turnover. In some situations, senior managers may not be aware of these issues because information from employees may not reach them directly. Problems may occur at lower levels of management but remain unnoticed by higher decision makers.

This learning is useful for my future career because it shows the importance of supportive leadership. If I work in a team or take a leadership role in the future, I will try to encourage open communication and trust among team members. Instead of controlling every detail, managers should guide employees and allow them to take responsibility for their work. This approach can help create a more positive and productive workplace.

Workplace Fairness and Employment Practices

Another important topic I learned about is workplace fairness and employment practices. Governments create employment laws and regulations to protect both employers and employees. These laws help ensure that workplaces operate fairly and that employees’ rights are respected.

Through this learning, I realised that organisations must balance business needs with employee wellbeing. Some companies use flexible employment arrangements such as casual or temporary roles. These arrangements can help businesses adjust their workforce depending on demand. However, they may also create uncertainty for employees who rely on stable income.

This topic helped me understand that fair treatment of employees is important for building a healthy workplace environment. When employees feel that they are treated fairly and respectfully, they are more likely to remain motivated and committed to their work. On the other hand, unfair practices may reduce trust between employees and employers.

In my future career, I plan to apply this learning by supporting fair and responsible workplace practices. Whether working in administrative roles or team environments, I will respect organisational policies while also considering employee wellbeing. By understanding both organisational needs and employee perspectives, I hope to contribute to a fair and balanced workplace environment.

Cultural Diversity in the Workplace

One important learning from my studies is the role of cultural diversity in modern workplaces. Many organisations today have employees from different countries, cultures, and language backgrounds. Cultural diversity can bring different ideas, perspectives, and experiences into an organisation.

From this topic, I learned that diversity can benefit organisations by encouraging creativity and innovation. When people from different backgrounds work together, they can share different ways of thinking and problem-solving. However, cultural differences can also create misunderstandings if people are not open to learning about other cultures.

This learning helped me understand the importance of inclusive workplaces. In some situations, recruitment practices may unintentionally favour people from certain cultural backgrounds or language groups. While organisations may require certain communication skills for specific roles, recruitment processes should still be fair and inclusive.

In my future career, I will try to apply this learning by respecting cultural differences and being open to different perspectives. Working with people from different backgrounds requires patience, understanding, and good communication. By supporting inclusive teamwork, organisations can create positive working environments where all employees feel valued and respected.

Balancing Organisational Performance and Employee Wellbeing

Another concept I learned during my studies is the importance of balancing organisational performance with employee wellbeing. Many businesses focus strongly on productivity, efficiency, and financial performance. While these goals are important, organisations must also consider the wellbeing of their employees.

Through this learning, I realised that employee wellbeing can influence organisational success. When employees feel supported, respected, and valued, they are more likely to remain motivated and perform well in their roles. On the other hand, workplaces with high stress or poor support systems may experience lower productivity and higher employee turnover.

This topic helped me understand that successful organisations often focus on both performance and employee welfare. Supportive leadership, clear communication, and fair workplace policies can help create a healthy work environment.

In my future career, I hope to apply this learning by supporting workplace environments that value both organisational goals and employee wellbeing. Even in administrative or support roles, employees can contribute to positive workplace culture through respectful communication and teamwork. By recognising the importance of both performance and wellbeing, organisations can achieve sustainable success while supporting the people who work within them.
